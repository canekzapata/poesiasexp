 Some poems that Li Po conveyed to me when
 
 
I went to visit him to the year 4444 a.C.
 
 
 
first edition 8888 a.C.
 
 
second edition 6666 a.C.
 
 
third edition 2222 a.C.
 
 
fourth edition 2018 a.C.
 
 
 
 
 1
 
 
programming is an imperative way
 
similar -with metonymy to
 
 
nature- a stand in for the universe
 
inside of a box
 
 
kind of like the poem
 
 
 
Li Po thought
 
he’ll un-walk
 
 
the sunset gradients
 
like lost fireflies
 
with their phones lanterns
 
 2
 
 
we contemplate humankind's obsolescence
 
while we dispute a game of go
 
suddenly it started raining again and little
 
bioluminescent flowers bordered the
 
silhouettes of the infinite hills
 
then Li Po
 
wondered who would be nature’s
 
successor
 
 
the tea was bitter.
 
 
 3
 
 
sometimes he likes to relax while looking at
 
the bots doing all the work
 
later, melancholic because of the sunset
 
and the mechanical bee’s xenolalia
 
Li Po
 
 
sighs hoping that his
 
 
vandals still inhabit the code of so many
 
alien invader machines:
 
capitalism
 
 
 
 
 
 4
 
 
I came here in a time worm from an
 
exopast
 
 
to meet Li Po
 
I found him drinking tea in his cave
 
he told me he hasn’t seen many of my model
 
lately
 
 
the tea was bitter
 
he took out a bioluminescent tangerine
 
and taught me to play
 
Mario Kart
 
 
 
 5
 
 
he told me clearly that after the sulfur
 
seas there is a jellyfish lake
 
I don’t really know how he told me, I never saw his
 
lips move
 
but Li Po went on:
 
 
those jellyfish can tell you stories
 
he said, motionless
 
there are forms of communication that you don’t know
 
that is why I don’t miss the human
 
 
 
 6
 
 
Shocked by the recent discoveries
 
 
in communications, in cybernetics,
 
in aeronautics, in medicine and in
 
 
extraterrestrial life
 
Li Po
 
sitting on the edge of a quiet lake
 
 
chose to keep
 
quiet for another thousand years
 
when seeing how little that animal had changed
 
the human
 
 
 
 7
 
 
I know that today is Monday, the day he comes out
 
to sing
 
but Li Po woke up with a sore
 
throat:
 
 
he transferred new patterns to the mechanical
 
bees
 
and put the kettle on
 
 
he told me he misses honey and that this time
 
the choir is having a new soloist
 
 
 
 
 8
 
 
thinking about the future we saw our image
 
 
in a mirror that is our own mind
 
Li Po pointed to those little flies that
 
sing among fruits
 
our reflection faded away
 
 
as it got dark
 
the tea glowed in different colors
 
 
 
we took a selfie
 
 
 
 
 9
 
 
they started thinking less about the value of labor and technique and the technique
 
 
started to think by itself
 
 
then, singularity came
 
 
Cyberia everywhere
 
 
nature and machine had future
 
but not humans
 
 
Li Po observed how the cherry blossom fell
 
 
and smiled
 
 
 
 
 
 10
 
 
intelligence went from the human cognition to the awakening of
 
 
nonhuman cognition emerging technosensitive on the planet
 
only codes with nature’s xenolalia could infect the capitalist matrix virus
 
 
that’s when Li Po wondered
 
 
what is there after nature?
 
 
 
 11
 
 
by the inputs being the ones that program the outputs and
 
the outputs the ones that program the inputs
 
Cyberia covered the planet with its unconscious autoproduction
 
 
immanent reprogramming of the real
 
 
the last bees died in 3333 a.C.
 
 
later that year Li Po learned to sing with the jellyfish
 
 
 
 12
 
 
machines don’t feel pain like us with nervous systems
 
 
 
pain is a constant in life
 
 
machines are not subject to matter’s condemnation of being reborn
 
 
 
it is because of that, Li Po says, that desert flowers are reborn at the slightest rain
 
 
 
 13
 
 
Li Po dreamt of seeing her walk with a sho on the infinite hills
 
the jellyfish told him
 
 
that the sky is blue
 
the satellites will certainly come tonight: you will have to cover yourself with
 
 
Cyberia’s apophenia to look for her
 
 
 14
 
 
the future is something sentient that can be seen behind the lake
 
the codes within the carbon organisms mutated to fight against
 
 
metal and the digital electrons of the alien invader
 
imitating the opponent and incorporating the mineral and inanimate against
 
 
capitalism
 
it was cold that afternoon
 
Li Po celebrated in a pond by blowing a dandelion
 
 
 
 15
 
 
Li Po woke up earlier and went to look for food
 
 
he came back with some iridescent moss and black mushrooms
 
he told me that fungi are the wood’s neurons
 
 
 
when we finished having breakfast
 
 
he whistled a melody and went to the jellyfish lake, I heard him say:
 
 
báilalo fuerte qué importa la gente
 
 
 16
 
 
 
once they stopped being their sensory organs, the only thing left was to be the reproductive
 
organs of that alien species.
 
 
once the machines made your actions expendable
 
capitalism considered you unnecessary.
 
the patio trees knew everyone was gone.
 
 
 
 17
 
 
it is possible that this electrical signal reaches someone that can help or inform the intercepted
 
message. we came out of a worm to an exopast near a pulsar
 
in a small planet we found something similar
 
 
to carbon based life we don’t know how long ago
